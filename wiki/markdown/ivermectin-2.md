#### Background on Ivermectin and Alcohol
Ivermectin is an antiparasitic medication approved by the FDA for treating conditions such as strongyloidiasis and onchocerciasis (river blindness), among others. It is also used off-label for conditions like scabies. Alcohol, on the other hand, is a common substance that can interact with various medications, potentially altering their effects or increasing side effects. The question of whether there are dangerous interactions between Ivermectin and alcohol is critical for ensuring patient safety, especially given the medication's widespread use.

#### Clinical Studies and Research
Research into the specific interaction includes studies focused on Ivermectin's potential use for alcohol use disorder (AUD). For instance, a pilot study published in *Alcoholism: Clinical and Experimental Research* (2016) examined the safety of Ivermectin (30 mg oral, QD) in combination with an intoxicating dose of alcohol. The results suggested that Ivermectin did not increase the number or severity of adverse effects during alcohol administration, indicating safety in this controlled setting. However, this study does not provide evidence for general use and is specific to AUD treatment, with no significant reduction in alcohol craving or reinforcing effects observed.

Another study from ScienceDirect (2013) investigated whether alcoholic beverages enhance the availability of Ivermectin, finding that severe clinical problems seen with Ivermectin are not associated with alcohol intake. This suggests that in certain contexts, such as mass drug administration programs, the interaction might not pose significant risks. However, these findings are context-specific and do not negate the general warnings from medical databases.
